About TKSS

A Chess-First Sports Institute with a Vision for the Future

Taanya Kaavya School of Sports (TKSS) is a chess-focused sports institute committed to developing young minds through structured, high-quality chess education. While chess is our primary focus today, our long-term vision is to grow into a multidisciplinary sports institute, offering opportunities for students to excel across various sports and skill-development programs.

Operating through our main centre in Mogappair and partner programs across Chennai, TKSS provides a supportive learning environment where every student is encouraged to think, learn, and grow with confidence.

At TKSS, we believe chess is more than a game. It is a powerful tool for developing focus, critical thinking, decision-making, patience, discipline, and resilience. Our coaching philosophy is student-centred, emphasizing individual growth, structured learning pathways, regular progress reviews, and long-term skill development rather than short-term results.

TKSS is proud to have trained students from India and internationally, including students in the United States who have successfully earned both US Chess ratings and FIDE ratings.

Curriculum Roadmap

Every stage has clear objectives, measurable skills, defined outcomes, and structured practice.

Absolute Beginner

Objective: Build confidence with the chessboard and basic rules.

Skills: Piece movement, capturing, check, checkmate, and simple game play.

Outcome: Play complete games independently while understanding the fundamental rules of chess.

Books · Worksheets · Fun Activities · Practice Assignments

Beginner

Objective: Develop strong chess fundamentals.

Skills: Opening principles, basic tactics, piece coordination, simple endgames, and notation.

Outcome: Play stronger practice games with improved tactical awareness and confidence.

Worksheets · Assignments · Guided Practice Games · Review Sessions

Advanced Beginner

Objective: Prepare for competitive chess.

Skills: Tactical patterns, checkmate themes, opening systems, endgame fundamentals, tournament rules, and game analysis.

Outcome: Participate confidently in tournaments with a solid understanding of competitive play.

Recommended Books · Analysis Exercises · Tournament Practice

Intermediate

Objective: Strengthen strategic understanding and competitive performance.

Skills: Positional planning, tactical calculation, opening repertoire development, middlegame strategy, and endgame technique.

Outcome: Consistently perform at a higher level in tournaments through deeper game understanding.

Homework · Model Games · Endgame Drills · Tournament Analysis

Advanced

Objective: Maximise individual performance through personalised coaching.

Skills: Advanced opening preparation, engine-assisted analysis, tournament planning, psychological preparation, and performance optimisation.

Outcome: Achieve long-term rating improvement and compete confidently at higher levels.

Personalised Curriculum · Performance Reviews · Individual Training Plans · Tournament Preparation

FAQ

Questions parents usually ask before joining.

Can absolute beginners join?

Yes. TKSS has an Absolute Beginner program for first-time learners.

What age can start chess?

Children from around age 5 can begin, depending on attention span and interest.

Do you offer online classes?

Yes. Online classes are available for India, USA, UK, Singapore, Malaysia, Australia, and other countries.

Are offline classes available?

Yes. Offline training is available at the TKSS main centre and selected partner locations.

What are the batch timings?

Classes are conducted on weekdays and weekends according to batch schedules.

Will students play tournaments?

Students are guided toward internal, school, district, state, national, and FIDE-rated tournaments when ready.

What chess materials are provided?

Students may receive worksheets, assignments, practice games, and level-wise study guidance.

Can we attend a demo class?

Yes. Use the Book Free Demo form and TKSS will guide you to the right batch.

What happens if a class is missed?

Missed-class support depends on batch schedule and availability. Contact TKSS for details.
